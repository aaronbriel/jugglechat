SARS-CoV-2 means Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2. It is the virus that leads to the coronavirus disease, also known as COVID-19. Unlike SARS, in COVID19 patients become highly infectious before they become seriously ill, explaining at least in part why Covid-19 acts like a super-SARS.  [[Would you like to know why there are two names for COVID19 (i.e. SARS-CoV-2 and COVID19)?]] [[What is the difference between SARS-CoV-2 and COVID19?]] SARS-CoV-2 refers to the virus that causes COVID-19. COVID-19 is the disease itself. The virus has a different name for the disease to support the development of diagnostic testing, vaccines, and medicines. The disease is named as such to enable discussion on disease prevention, spread, its ability to transmit, its severity, and its treatment.
